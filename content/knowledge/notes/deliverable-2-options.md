## Executive Summary

This document presents six distinct commercial engagement models for the IDIRNET × LIGHTHEART relationship. Each option addresses the fundamental tension between Brendan Dillon's desire for control and commitment and Kev Freeney's need to preserve IDIRNET autonomy and existing practice.

### Key Constraints from Sources
- Brendan assumed 5 days/week PAYE employment
- Kev has existing practice + legacy clients (Diageo, Jon Hopkins, etc.)
- Brendan wants April 1st start, "no time to lose"
- Equity in Lightheart (not IDIRNET) via share options
- Brendan retains curation control
- IDIRNET is "complementary" but may work with "competing ventures"

### What Each Party Gets

**Lightheart Gets:**
- Full control: "landlord and tenant and decision maker"
- Dedicated bandwidth: 5 days/week guaranteed commitment
- IP ownership: All work product becomes company property
- Confidentiality: Standard employee confidentiality obligations
- Non-compete: Broader restrictions possible under employment

**IDIRNET Gets:**
- Cashflow certainty: Regular salary (amount TBD)
- Equity: Share options vesting annually
- Case study rights: Limited - work owned by Lightheart
- Network access: Through Kev's position

3. **Control Friction**
   - Failure: Kev's Creative Director title conflicts with Brendan's "acting Head of Marketing" role
   - Mitigation: Clear RACI matrix; separation of roles when negotiating (Laura interface)

- **LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED:** Employee IP assignment vs. background IP protection
- **LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED:** Post-employment restrictions scope

### Decision Rights (Evolving)

| Phase | Kev/IDIRNET Authority | Lightheart Authority |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Phase 1 | Creative Direction, tech advice | Curation, equipment, budget |
| Phase 2 | Technical delivery, QC | Strategic direction, partnerships |
| Phase 3 | Board representation (if equity) | Majority control (Brendan) |

### What Each Party Gets

**Lightheart Gets:**
- Immediate start: Phase 1 can begin April 1st
- Reduced commitment: Can evaluate before deeper engagement
- Proof of concept: Test IDIRNET delivery before partnership
- Flexibility: Exit points at each phase transition

**IDIRNET Gets:**
- Immediate cashflow: Phase 1 retainer provides income

- Partnership path: Clear route to equity participation

### Failure Modes

2. **Scope Creep Across Phases**
   - Failure: Phase 1 work expands without Phase 2 contract in place

3. **IDIRNET Formation Delay**
   - Failure: IDIRNET not incorporated by Phase 2 start

### When This Works
- Brendan accepts phased approach vs. immediate full commitment
- IDIRNET can incorporate within 3 months

- Kev accepts individual IR35 risk for Phase 1
