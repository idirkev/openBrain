# DELIVERABLE 5: Conflict, Confidentiality & Non-Compete Framework

**Prepared by:** Agent 4 (Risk & Legal Analyst)  
**Date:** February 28, 2026  
**Context:** IDIRNET × LIGHTHEART Legal Architecture

---

## Executive Summary

This deliverable presents two variants of the confidentiality and restrictive covenant framework for the IDIRNET × LIGHTHEART engagement, reflecting the divergent risk appetites of the parties:

- **Brendan Dillon (Lightheart)** seeks protection against competition, divided loyalty, and IP leakage
- **Kev Freeney (IDIRNET)** requires flexibility to maintain his existing practice and grow IDIRNET as a cultural systems studio

### Key Source Positions

> **Brendan acknowledges:** "If we go for (b) or (c) we'd still need a clause in the agreement on confidentiality but we would maybe have less need for non-compete clauses"

> **Kev requests:** "Narrow, reasonable restrictions tailored to Light Heart's legitimate interests, rather than broad non-compete language"

> **Brendan's concern:** "Idirnet is also likely to be involved with competing ventures"

---

## Variant A: Lightheart-Friendly Framework

### Rationale

This variant prioritizes Lightheart's commercial interests and reflects Brendan's concerns about:
- Competition
- Divided loyalty
- Loss of control
- IP leakage
- Strategic dilution

It assumes Option (a) PAYE employment or a tightly controlled Option (b) contract arrangement.

---

### 1. Confidential Information Definition (BROAD)

**Definition Scope:**

"Confidential Information" means all information disclosed by Lightheart to the Contractor/Employee, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

| Category | Includes |
|----------|----------|
| **Strategic Information** | Business plans, expansion strategies, partnership negotiations, funding discussions, investor communications |
| **Operational Information** | Venue specifications, technical infrastructure designs, equipment configurations, supplier relationships, pricing structures, operational procedures |
| **Financial Information** | Revenue projections, cost structures, profit margins, equity arrangements, financial performance data |
| **Creative Information** | Artist negotiations, show concepts, curation strategies, programming schedules, content adaptation methodologies |
| **Technical IP** | Software configurations, projection mapping techniques, pipeline integration methods, quality control frameworks, system documentation |
| **Third-Party Information** | Information received from partners, artists, suppliers, or investors under confidentiality obligations |

**Exclusions:**
- Information already in the public domain (other than by breach of this agreement)
- Information independently developed by IDIRNET without use of Lightheart's Confidential Information
- Information rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions

**Source Basis:** Brendan's concern about "IP leakage" and "strategic dilution"; acknowledgment that confidentiality clauses are needed in all engagement models

---

### 2. Non-Compete Covenant (12-Month Post-Termination)

**Restriction Period:** 12 months from termination of engagement

**Restricted Activities:**

During the restriction period, the Contractor/Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:

| Restriction | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Competing Venue Operation** | Own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, or provide services to any immersive entertainment venue, digital art gallery, or cultural venue within a 50km radius of Dublin city centre that competes with Lightheart's offerings |
| **Competing Services** | Provide technical system design, pipeline integration, operational modelling, or quality control frameworks to any venue that directly competes with Lightheart |
| **Artist Poaching** | Solicit, entice, or encourage any artist, performer, or content creator who has exhibited or performed at Lightheart within the 12 months preceding termination to transfer their engagement to a competing venue |
| **Staff Poaching** | Solicit, entice, or encourage any employee, contractor, or consultant of Lightheart to terminate their engagement |
| **Use of Trade Secrets** | Use or disclose any Confidential Information for the purpose of establishing, operating, or promoting a competing business |

**Geographic Scope:** Republic of Ireland, with primary focus on Dublin and surrounding counties (Dublin, Wicklow, Kildare, Meath, Louth)

**⚠️ LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED:** Non-compete clauses of this duration and scope require careful drafting to ensure enforceability under Irish law. Courts may strike down restrictions that are broader than necessary to protect legitimate business interests.

---

### 3. New Client Approval Regime

**Prior Approval Required:**

During the engagement period, the Contractor/Employee must obtain prior written approval from Lightheart's Board (or Brendan Dillon as designated representative) before:

(a) Accepting any new client engagement that involves:
   - Immersive venue design or operation
   - Technical infrastructure for entertainment venues
   - Digital art curation or programming
   - Projection mapping or AV system design for commercial venues

(b) Providing services to any entity that operates or plans to operate within the live entertainment, immersive experience, or digital art exhibition sectors in Ireland

(c) Engaging with any artist, performer, or content creator who has been introduced to or discovered through Lightheart activities

**Approval Process:**
- Written request submitted at least 14 days before intended engagement
- Lightheart to respond within 7 days (silence = deemed approval)
- Approval may be withheld only on reasonable grounds related to conflict of interest
- Approved engagements documented in writing

**Source Basis:** Kev's request for "A simple disclosure/approval process for any new work that could overlap with Light Heart"; Brendan's concern about competing ventures

---

### 4. Clean Room Protocols for Sensitive Projects

**Clean Room Requirements:**

For projects involving Lightheart's core proprietary technology, strategic partnerships, or pre-launch content:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Physical Separation** | IDIRNET personnel working on sensitive projects shall work from designated Lightheart premises or secure facilities |
| **Information Segregation** | Confidential Information shall be stored on separate systems with access controls, encryption, and audit trails |
| **Personnel Screening** | Individuals with access to highly sensitive information shall sign enhanced confidentiality undertakings |
| **No Cross-Contamination** | Personnel working on sensitive Lightheart projects shall not work on competing projects during the same period without 30-day "cooling off" periods |
| **Documentation Control** | All work product shall be clearly marked, version-controlled, and subject to Lightheart's document management protocols |
| **Exit Protocols** | Upon project completion or termination, all Confidential Information shall be returned or destroyed with written certification |

**Source Basis:** Brendan's concern about "IP leakage"; his statement that "we would be a lot more cautious about sharing future plans" under Options (b) or (c)

---

### 5. Legacy Client Carve-Out (NARROW)

**Permitted Activities:**

Notwithstanding the above restrictions, the Contractor/Employee may continue to service the following pre-existing client relationships:

(a) **Named Legacy Clients:**
   - Diageo
   - Jon Hopkins
   - Sounds from the Safe Harbor
   - Open Ear
   - All Together Now

(b) **Scope Limitation:** Services to legacy clients shall be limited to the scope and nature of services provided prior to the Lightheart engagement commencement date

(c) **No Expansion:** The Contractor/Employee shall not expand services to legacy clients into areas that directly compete with Lightheart's offerings without prior approval

(d) **Quarterly Reporting:** The Contractor/Employee shall provide quarterly reports to Lightheart detailing work performed for legacy clients

**Source Basis:** Kev's request for "A clear carve-out for my existing practice and legacy clients as Kev Freeney"; specific named clients listed

## Variant B: IDIRNET-Friendly Framework

### Rationale

This variant prioritizes IDIRNET's need for operational flexibility and reflects Kev's position that IDIRNET is "not a competing venue" but "a cultural systems studio". It is designed for Option (c) partnership agreement or a loosely structured Option (b) contract.

---

### 1. Confidential Information Definition (NARROW)

**Definition Scope:**

"Confidential Information" means only information that:

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Documented Trade Secrets** | Is expressly identified in writing by Lightheart as "Confidential" or "Trade Secret" at the time of disclosure or within 14 days thereafter |
| **Technical Specifications** | Specific technical configurations, equipment settings, and proprietary software code developed specifically for Lightheart and not using industry-standard methodologies |
| **Financial Terms** | Specific pricing, cost structures, and financial terms of partnership agreements with named artists or content producers |
| **Strategic Plans** | Specific, documented expansion plans, funding negotiations, or partnership discussions that have not been publicly announced |

**Explicit Exclusions:**

The following shall NOT constitute Confidential Information:

| Exclusion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry Knowledge** | General industry practices, technical standards, and methodologies commonly used in immersive entertainment and digital art |
| **Background IP** | Methods, processes, and techniques brought by IDIRNET to the engagement (as distinguished from outputs created specifically for Lightheart) |
| **Public Information** | Information already in the public domain or subsequently published without breach of this agreement |
| **Independent Development** | Information independently developed by IDIRNET without reference to Lightheart's Confidential Information |
| **Prior Knowledge** | Information already known to IDIRNET prior to disclosure by Lightheart |

**Source Basis:** Kev's request for "Clear distinction between methods/processes I bring (background IP) and outputs created specifically for Lightheart (foreground IP)"; Brendan's acknowledgment that Options (b) and (c) require "less need for non-compete clauses"

---

### 2. No Non-Compete; Limited Non-Solicitation

**No Non-Compete Covenant:**

IDIRNET and its personnel are expressly permitted to:

(a) Work with any other clients, including immersive venues, cultural institutions, and entertainment operators

(b) Provide technical system design, pipeline integration, and operational modelling services to any entity, including those that may compete with Lightheart

(c) Develop and deploy similar methodologies, frameworks, and processes for other clients (recognising that "a process is a process that will always have different outcomes")

(d) Operate IDIRNET as a cultural systems studio serving multiple clients and venues

**Limited Non-Solicitation (Lightheart Artists Only):**

During the engagement period and for 6 months thereafter, IDIRNET shall not:

(a) Directly solicit artists who are under exclusive exhibition agreements with Lightheart for the purpose of diverting them to competing venues

(b) Use Lightheart's Confidential Information (as narrowly defined) to target artists for competing engagements

**Permitted Activities:**

The following are expressly permitted:
- General marketing and business development activities not targeting specific Lightheart artists
- Responding to unsolicited approaches from artists
- Working with artists who have independently established relationships with IDIRNET
- Providing services to artists who have completed their engagement with Lightheart

**Source Basis:** Kev's request for "Narrow, reasonable restrictions tailored to Light Heart's legitimate interests, rather than broad non-compete language"; IDIRNET positioning as "not a competing venue" but "a cultural systems studio"

---

### 3. Notification-Only Regime for New Clients

**Notification Requirement:**

IDIRNET shall provide quarterly written notification to Lightheart of:

(a) New client engagements in the immersive entertainment, digital art, or cultural venue sectors

(b) The general nature of services being provided (without disclosing confidential details)

(c) Confirmation that the engagement does not involve use of Lightheart's Confidential Information (as narrowly defined)

**No Approval Required:**

Lightheart shall have no right to approve or veto IDIRNET's client engagements, provided that:

(a) IDIRNET does not use Lightheart's Confidential Information (as narrowly defined)

(b) IDIRNET does not breach the limited non-solicitation obligations

(c) IDIRNET maintains appropriate confidentiality protections

**Conflict Resolution:**

If Lightheart believes a new client engagement creates a conflict of interest:

(a) Lightheart shall notify IDIRNET in writing within 14 days of the quarterly notification

(b) The parties shall discuss in good faith to determine if a genuine conflict exists

(c) If agreement cannot be reached, the matter shall be escalated to senior representatives for resolution

**Source Basis:** Kev's request for "A simple disclosure/approval process for any new work that could overlap with Light Heart"; IDIRNET's need to operate as an independent cultural systems studio serving multiple clients

---

### 4. Legacy Client Carve-Out (BROAD)

**Named Legacy Clients:**

IDIRNET and Kev Freeney may continue and expand services to the following pre-existing clients without restriction:

- Diageo
- Jon Hopkins
- Sounds from the Safe Harbor
- Open Ear
- All Together Now

**Pre-2026 Clients:**

In addition to the named clients, IDIRNET may continue services to any client relationship established prior to January 1, 2026, provided that:

(a) The relationship is documented (contract, invoice, or email correspondence)

(b) The scope of services is consistent with the pre-existing engagement

(c) Quarterly notification is provided to Lightheart

**Expansion Permitted:**

IDIRNET may expand services to legacy clients into new areas, including:

- Technical system design and implementation
- Pipeline integration and content adaptation
- Operational modelling and quality control
- Artist support and curation services

**Source Basis:** Kev's request for "A clear carve-out for my existing practice and legacy clients as Kev Freeney"; specific named clients

---

### 5. Clean Room Protocols (MINIMAL)

**Standard Confidentiality:**

IDIRNET shall maintain standard industry confidentiality practices:

(a) Access to Lightheart's Confidential Information (as narrowly defined) shall be limited to personnel with a need to know

(b) Confidential Information shall be stored securely with appropriate access controls

(c) Upon termination, Confidential Information shall be returned or destroyed as requested

**No Enhanced Protocols:**

No clean room, physical separation, or enhanced screening requirements shall apply, except where:

(a) Expressly agreed in writing for specific, identified projects

(b) Required by third-party confidentiality obligations (e.g., artist contracts)

(c) Mandated by applicable law or regulation

**Source Basis:** IDIRNET positioning as an independent cultural systems studio; Brendan's acknowledgment that Options (b) and (c) require "less need for non-compete clauses"

## Trade-Off Analysis

| Factor | Variant A (Lightheart-Friendly) | Variant B (IDIRNET-Friendly) | Middle Ground Recommendation |
|--------|--------------------------------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Control** | HIGH - Lightheart maintains tight control over Kev/IDIRNET's activities, client relationships, and competitive exposure | LOW - IDIRNET retains full operational independence and client selection freedom | MODERATE - Notification regime with limited approval rights for direct competitors only; 6-month non-compete for key personnel |
| **Flexibility** | LOW - Significant constraints on IDIRNET's ability to serve other clients and grow the business | HIGH - IDIRNET can operate as independent cultural systems studio serving multiple clients | MODERATE - IDIRNET free to serve non-competing clients; approval required only for direct venue competitors in Dublin area |
| **Enforceability** | MODERATE - Broad non-compete may be challenged under Irish law; 12-month duration at upper limit of enforceability | HIGH - Narrow definitions and limited restrictions more likely to be upheld; aligns with IDIRNET's legitimate business model | HIGH - Balanced approach more likely to be viewed as protecting legitimate interests without undue restraint |
| **Risk of IP Leakage** | LOW - Clean room protocols and broad confidentiality definition minimise exposure | MODERATE - Narrower definitions create some risk, but background/foreground IP distinction provides clarity | MODERATE-LOW - Documented trade secret definition with background IP carve-out balances protection and practicality |
| **IDIRNET Growth Potential** | LOW - Restrictions may limit IDIRNET's ability to attract investment and scale | HIGH - Unrestricted growth potential; IDIRNET can serve multiple clients and build cultural systems studio model | MODERATE - Growth permitted with transparency; Lightheart has visibility but not veto over client base |
| **Brendan's Concerns Addressed** | HIGH - Directly addresses competition, divided loyalty, loss of control, IP leakage, strategic dilution | LOW-MODERATE - Addresses concerns through positioning and transparency, but less direct control | MODERATE-HIGH - Addresses core concerns while preserving IDIRNET viability; requires trust-building |
| **Kev's Requirements Met** | LOW - Significant constraints on existing practice and IDIRNET development | HIGH - Full carve-out for legacy clients; no non-compete; operational independence | MODERATE-HIGH - Legacy clients protected; limited non-compete; notification rather than approval regime |
| **IR35 Compliance** | LOW - Tight control indicators increase IR35 risk for Option (b) | HIGH - Independent operation supports genuine B2B relationship | HIGH - Balanced approach supports independent contractor status |
| **Legal Cost** | MODERATE - Complex drafting required; higher litigation risk if challenged | LOW - Simpler drafting; lower litigation risk | MODERATE - Requires careful drafting to achieve balance |

---

## Middle Ground Recommendation

### Recommended Approach for Negotiation:

1. **Confidential Information:** Narrow definition focusing on documented trade secrets and specific financial/strategic information, with explicit background IP carve-out

2. **Non-Compete:** 6-month post-termination restriction limited to direct venue operation within 25km of Dublin (not services provision); no restriction on IDIRNET's cultural systems studio activities

3. **Non-Solicitation:** 6-month restriction on soliciting Lightheart artists under exclusive agreement; no restriction on general marketing or responding to unsolicited approaches

4. **New Clients:** Notification-only regime with 14-day objection period; Lightheart may object only on specific conflict grounds; disputes resolved through escalation

5. **Legacy Clients:** Full carve-out for named clients (Diageo, Jon Hopkins, Sounds from the Safe Harbor, Open Ear, All Together Now) plus pre-2026 clients with documentation

6. **Clean Room:** Standard confidentiality protocols; enhanced protocols only for specific, identified sensitive projects agreed in writing

## Legal Review Requirements

### LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED for the following elements:

1. **Non-compete enforceability:** Any non-compete clause exceeding 6-12 months or extending beyond direct competition may be unenforceable under Irish and EU competition law.

2. **Background IP definition:** The distinction between "methods/processes I bring" and "outputs created specifically for Lightheart" requires careful drafting to avoid disputes.

3. **Legacy client scope:** The carve-out for "existing practice and legacy clients" should be clearly defined to prevent scope creep disputes.

4. **Confidentiality duration:** Perpetual confidentiality for trade secrets vs. time-limited confidentiality for other information requires distinction.

### TAX REVIEW REQUIRED for the following elements:

1. **IR35 implications:** Variant A's tight control regime increases risk of deemed employment status for Option (b); Variant B's independence supports contractor status.

2. **Equity vesting:** Share option structure with annual vesting requires tax-efficient structuring.

3. **Retainer vs. employment income:** Rolling retainer of EUR 12,000/month must be structured to avoid PAYE obligations if Option (b) or (c) selected.

## Conclusion

The confidentiality and restrictive covenant framework presents a fundamental tension between:

1. **Brendan's position** acknowledges that Options (b) and (c) require less restrictive covenants, but his underlying concerns about competition and control remain

2. **Kev's position** seeks narrow, reasonable restrictions with full legacy client protection and clear IP distinctions

3. **The middle ground** offers a balanced approach that addresses Lightheart's legitimate interests while preserving IDIRNET's viability as a cultural systems studio

### Recommendation

**Proceed with middle ground framework as the opening negotiation position, with Variant B elements as fallback and Variant A elements reserved for specific, identified risk scenarios.**
